\section{Conclusion}

We presented \textit{private data collections} (PDCs): private data is stored only on authorized peers while its cryptographic hash is recorded on the replicated ledger. A transient store, hashed read-write sets, and a gossip-based dissemination protocol together deliver privacy, atomicity, integrity, and auditability within a single shared ledger---without specialized hardware, expensive cryptographic primitives, or the management overhead that makes channel-based deployments brittle at scale. Our Hyperledger Fabric implementation has been production-ready since v1.2 and achieves throughput and latency comparable to standard public transactions. Deployments at Walmart, MediLedger, GSBN, and MineHub confirm that the design generalizes across industries with very different trust models and data-sharing patterns. More broadly, PDCs demonstrate that selective transparency---giving designated parties verifiable access to exactly the data they are authorized to see---is achievable within a standard permissioned blockchain architecture, opening a practical path for regulated industries that have so far found blockchain's transparency model incompatible with their compliance obligations.
