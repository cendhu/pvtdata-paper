The full proactive dissemination procedure is given in Algorithm~\ref{alg:dissemination} (\S\ref{s:design}), which executes on the endorsing peer immediately after simulation. Two tunable parameters govern its behavior: $n_{required}$, the number of acknowledgments the endorser blocks on before returning its signed response to the client, and $n_{dissem}$, the total number of peers targeted by a subsequent non-blocking background phase. Separating these parameters lets operators balance endorsement latency against data availability independently. Setting $n_{dissem}$ aggressively ensures most collection members receive the data proactively, minimizing commit-time reactive pulls; keeping $n_{required}$ small protects endorsement latency. When the endorser cannot obtain $n_{required}$ acknowledgments before the timeout, it returns a failure to the client, who may retry with different endorsers.
