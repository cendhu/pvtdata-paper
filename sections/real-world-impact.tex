\section{Real-World Impact}

Since its introduction in Hyperledger Fabric v1.2, the private data collection mechanism has seen broad adoption across industries with distinct privacy requirements. MineHub~\cite{minehub} deploys PDCs to isolate bilateral contract data across thousands of supply-chain participants in mining and metals, replacing the coarse-grained channel model that could not scale to dynamic data-sharing relationships. The IBM Food Trust---deployed at Walmart and other major retailers~\cite{7-to-2, walmart-hf}---protects supplier-specific pricing and process data with PDCs while preserving end-to-end traceability for food safety. GSBN (Global Shipping Business Network)~\cite{gsbn} relies on PDCs to keep private trade documents such as Bills of Lading confidential within its permissioned shipping network.

The pharmaceutical sector illustrates a particularly instructive use case. The MediLedger project~\cite{mediledger-fda, mediledger-hf} combines PDCs with zero-knowledge proofs for drug tracking under the U.S. Drug Supply Chain Security Act (DSCSA), an approach validated by an FDA pilot program; this confirms that PDCs can serve as a substrate for layering additional cryptographic guarantees when the threat model demands them. In aerospace, Honeywell's GoDirect Trade marketplace~\cite{honeywell} uses both channels and private data collections to isolate transaction data in its B2B marketplace for used aircraft parts. In financial services, Oracle's Blockchain Platform~\cite{oracle-cbdc} stores bank account numbers solely in the private data of participating organizations while recording one-way hashes on the shared ledger, protecting account attributes in a wholesale Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC) solution.

Across all of these deployments the underlying pattern is the same: PDCs partition sensitive bilateral data---pricing, contracts, account identifiers---while preserving a shared, hash-verified audit trail that satisfies regulatory compliance and cross-party verification requirements.
