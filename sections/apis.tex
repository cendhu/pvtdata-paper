\textbf{Example.} A smart contract executes:
\texttt{GetState}($k_1$) $\to$ \texttt{PutState}($k_1$, $v_2$) $\to$ \texttt{GetPrivateData}($c_1$, $k_2$) $\to$ \texttt{GetPrivateData}($c_2$, $k_3$) $\to$ \texttt{PutPrivateData}($c_1$, $k_2$, $v_3$) $\to$ \texttt{PutPrivateData}($c_2$, $k_3$, $v_4$). \\[2pt]
This produces three partitioned outputs: \textbf{Public RW-Set:} R--$\{k_1, \text{ver}\}$, W--$\{k_1, v_2\}$. \quad \textbf{Hashed RW-Set:} R--$\{c_1, H(k_2), \text{ver}\}$, $\{c_2, H(k_3), \text{ver}\}$; W--$\{c_1, H(k_2), H(v_3)\}$, $\{c_2, H(k_3), H(v_4)\}$. \quad \textbf{Private Write-Sets:} Collection $c_1$: W--$\{k_2, v_3\}$; Collection $c_2$: W--$\{k_3, v_4\}$.
